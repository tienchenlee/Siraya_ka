## Diagnostic Rule
Classify 'ka' as a **coordinator (and)** if it links two or more clauses that can each stand alone as grammatical sentences, and the argument structure of each predicate is independently satisfied.

## Analysis
Although 'ka' in Siraya has multiple functions, its role as a **coordinator** can be distinguished by examining the argument structure of the clauses it connects. When 'ka' functions as a coordinator meaning 'and', it links two or more independent clauses that could each stand alone as a complete sentence. In other words, the argument structure of each clause is independently fulfilled.

'Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven.' (Matthew 5:16)

In (1), the syntactic structure of the clauses conjoined by ka demonstrates parallelism in both argument structure and clause composition. The first clause contains an explicit nominative subject 'light your.PL', while the second and third clauses exhibit pro-drop, where the subject is omitted but can be inferred from context. Despite this variation, all three clauses maintain the same underlying structure: a predicate, an overt or a null subject, and an adjunct prepositional phrases or object NP containing a relative clause.

Each clause can function independently, as illustrated in (2)–(4). All remain grammatical without the other clauses, demonstrating that 'ka' is coordinating separate clauses rather than introducing a dependent clause.
