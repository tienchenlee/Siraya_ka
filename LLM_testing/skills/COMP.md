## Diagnostic Rule
Classify 'ka' as a **complementizer (COMP)** if the clause introduced by 'ka' satisfies the argument requirement of the matrix verb. The verb selects a clause as its complement, and removing the ka-clause results in an incomplete or ungrammatical sentence.

## Analysis
Although 'ka' in Siraya has multiple functions, its role as a **complementizer** can be distinguished by examining the argument structure of the matrix predicate. A ka-clause that **satisfies the verb's valency requirement** signals a complement clause, meaning the clause is required by the matrix verb.

## Example
(1) when see-LV they.GEN ka PC.already.AV-PFV die.AV  
'But when they came to Jesus, and saw that he was dead already.' (John 19:33)

In (1), the verb 'see' does not have its argument structure satisfied by two NPs. This suggests that 'see-LV' in this construction **selects for a CP**. Removing the ka-clause renders the sentence ungrammatical, as shown in (2):

(2) *see-LV they.GEN  
'They saw.'

This shows that the ka-clause in (1) is **required by the matrix predicate**, providing strong evidence that 'ka' functions as a **complementizer** introducing a CP.
