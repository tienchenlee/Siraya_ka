## Diagnostic Rule
Classify 'ka' as a **relativizer (REL)** if the clause introduced by 'ka' modifies a noun phrase rather than fulfilling the argument requirement of the verb. The matrix verb already has its required arguments satisfied, and removing the ka-clause leaves the sentence grammatical.

## Analysis
Although 'ka' in Siraya has multiple functions, its role as a **relativizer** can be distinguished by examining the argument structure of the matrix predicate. A ka-clause that **provides additional descriptive information about the head noun**, rather than satisfying the verb's valency, signals a relative clause.

## Example
(1) PAST-see-LV I.GEN NOM Spirit ka come.down.AV from.far.above.AV  
same.AV OBL dove abide.AV be.upon.AV him-OBL  
'I saw the Spirit descending from heaven like a dove, and it abode upon him.' (John 1:32)

In (1), two NPs 'I.GEN' and 'NOM Spirit' satisfy the valency requirement of the verb 'see'. Therefore, the clause introduced by 'ka' is **not a required complement**. If 'ka' were a complementizer, omitting the clause would result in ungrammaticality. However, sentence (2) remains syntactically correct:

(2) PAST-see-LV I.GEN NOM Spirit  
'I saw the Spirit.'

This shows that the ka-clause in (1) contributes additional descriptive information about 'NOM Spirit' and is best analyzed as a **relative clause** modifying the head noun.
